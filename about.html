The story

Why this guide exists

When my family arrived in Canada, we started from scratch — just like every other immigrant family. Which school board? What is OSSD? How does OHIP work? What is a TFSA? The questions were endless and the answers were scattered across a dozen government websites, WhatsApp groups, and forum threads.

My daughters — one in Grade 5, one in Grade 8 — needed me to understand the Ontario school system fast. Not someday. Right now. Grade 8 is when course choices start to matter. Community hours need to be tracked. Streams need to be understood. I learned all of this the hard way, piece by piece.

After about six months of figuring it out, I realised I had built something in my head that other families would genuinely benefit from. So I turned it into a website. Not a business. Not a service. Just a fellow immigrant sharing what took a year to learn — so the next family doesn't have to spend that year confused.

Everything on this site is what I actually researched, verified, and in most cases — lived through with my own family in Cornwall, Eastern Ontario.

The philosophy

What this site is — and what it isn't

✅
Lived experience
Every page covers something my family actually navigated — school registration, TFSA setup, healthcare, travel, community hours. All real.
🎯
Practical over theoretical
Specific steps, real numbers, actual timelines. The kind of information you need when you arrive — not a year later.
🤝
Community, not commerce
Free, always. The goal is to reduce the overwhelm every new family feels in their first year in Canada.
Important: Everything on this site is shared for general educational purposes — not professional legal, financial, or medical advice.
