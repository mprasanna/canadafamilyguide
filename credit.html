What builds your score — and where you stand
The most important factor: Payment history (35% of your score). One missed payment can drop your score by 60–100 points. Always pay at least the minimum — ideally the full balance.
Your month-by-month credit journey
The 3 rules that matter most
30%
Utilisation limit
Never use more than 30% of your credit limit. If your limit is $500, keep balance under $150. This single rule has the biggest impact after payment history.
100%
Pay in full
Pay your full balance every month — not just the minimum. Minimum payments keep you in debt and cost you interest. Full payment = zero interest + score improvement.
6mo
Before applying again
Every credit application creates a "hard inquiry" that drops your score slightly. Wait 6 months between applications. Don't apply for multiple cards at once.
Which cards to get — in order
Newcomer tip: All major Canadian banks have newcomer packages that include a secured credit card with no credit history required. Go to a branch — don't apply online for your first card. Bring your passport, SIN, and work permit or PR card.
Mistakes that set you back months
✗
Applying for too many cards at once. Each application is a hard inquiry. Three applications in one month signals desperation to lenders and drops your score. One card at a time, 6 months apart.
✗
Closing your oldest card. Length of credit history is 15% of your score. Your oldest card is your most valuable — keep it open and use it occasionally, even after upgrading.
✗
Only paying the minimum. Minimum payments don't hurt your score directly, but they cost you interest and signal low financial health. Always pay in full.
✗
Using your card as a debit card. Running your card to the limit then paying it off looks bad — lenders see a snapshot of your balance. Keep it under 30% of your limit at all times.
