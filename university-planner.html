Out-of-Province Universities Worth Knowing

Many Ontario students — especially newcomers — overlook strong out-of-province options. These schools accept OUAC applications and your Ontario prerequisites apply.

McGill University
Montréal, QC
World-ranked. Strong medicine, engineering, law. Quebec tuition is lower for out-of-province than most assume (~$9,000–12,000/yr vs $7,000 for QC residents). English instruction available across most programs.
Admission avg: 85–94% depending on program
UBC — University of British Columbia
Vancouver, BC
Top 5 in Canada. Excellent for sciences, engineering, forestry, and international business. Vancouver location has tech industry connections. Out-of-province tuition higher (~$7,000–9,000/yr more than BC residents).
Admission avg: 83–93% depending on program
University of Alberta
Edmonton, AB
Top 5 research university. Strong engineering, science, and business. Alberta has no provincial income tax — living costs after graduation are significantly better. Out-of-province tuition similar to Ontario.
Admission avg: 78–88% depending on program
Dalhousie University
Halifax, NS
Strong medicine, law, engineering, and ocean sciences. Halifax is affordable and growing. Atlantic Canada immigration pathway (AIPP) makes it attractive for long-term settlement. Lower living costs than Ontario.
Admission avg: 75–85% depending on program
Key rule for all out-of-province schools: Your Ontario course prerequisites still apply. McGill Engineering still requires MCV4U and SPH4U. Your Grade Planner above is valid for any Canadian university. Apply through OUAC 105 for out-of-province.
Admission averages are typical ranges based on recent cycles — they change annually. Always verify on the university's official admissions page. Not official educational advice.
